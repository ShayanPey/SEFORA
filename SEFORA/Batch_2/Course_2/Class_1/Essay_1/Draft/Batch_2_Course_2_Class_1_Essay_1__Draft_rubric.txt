Cross-cultural Narrative_Rough Draft


Your rough draft should be at least two pages (600 words) 
	with one inch margins; 
	in Times New Roman 12pt font; 
	double spaced. 
No research is necessary for this assignment. 

You will write a cross-cultural narrative, describing:
	how you once imagined a place in Pittsburgh before you saw it 
		(e.g., somewhere on the Secret Pittsburgh map),
	what you discovered or realized about it when you ultimately arrived,
	how the place was similar to or different from a place in your home country, 
	and why there may be a gap between your preconception and realization. 

You should include words and phrases from both your first language (L1) and second language (L2) 
	in your cross-cultural narrative and digital story. 
You will also use DALLE (Bing search) to generate images that complement the written essay. 

Please use the following questions to brainstorm ideas for your place narrative:


Main Point: 
	What are the key messages you want to convey through the narrative?

	What was your initial imagination of this place based on its name or description? 
	What did you expect to see or experience? 
	What were the key elements of your preconception?

Rising action:

	In what ways were they similar, and where did you observe notable differences?
