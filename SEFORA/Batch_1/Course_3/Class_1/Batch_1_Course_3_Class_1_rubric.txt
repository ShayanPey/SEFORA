Common Issues in Intro to Writing Fiction First Drafts


	Common Issues in Intro to Writing Fiction First Drafts 


	Repeated spelling errors  

	Repeated grammatical errors (for example, “my Mother” and “I called mom” happens frequently)  

	Total avoidance of using dialogue in any way (I know from class discussions that this is often because the student isn’t sure how to punctuate dialogue)  

	Incorrect formatting (no indentations, skipping a line between paragraphs, etc.)  

	Run-on sentences (I don’t forbid this, as it sometimes reflects a style choice, but usually…not.)  

	Character’s name changes halfway through the story or begins to be spelled differently 

	Similarly, the character may begin as a full name and then progress to be being referred to as a nickname (Margaret —> Meg) over the draft  

	Story takes place in a hospital, police precinct or courtroom (all are specifically forbidden in my syllabus)  

	Story is straight genre (esp. Fantasy or mystery) 

	Story opens with a character waking up but without any particular narrative necessity  

	First section of story is throat-clearing, story’s plot actually begins with the second section 

	Large swaths of backstory, which were necessary for the author to figure out what she’s writing, but not of narrative need  

	Events happen without a time trigger or reason (I ask “Why did this happen today?” frequently)  

	Story is written in homage to a specific writer (generally Hemingway or Joyce), blocking my ability to see the writer’s own style 

	Story takes place in a coffee shop (this is okay, I just see it a lot!)  

	Story opens with a long description of the setting, and then that setting never appears in the story again.  

	Verb tense shifts and changes throughout the draft (without awareness, just in error) 

	Story is told in one specific point of view but there are occasional slips into a different POV (e.g. shifting from third to first person without awareness) 

	Writer has chosen to write about a world they have no personal experience in 

	Main character is depressed and spends a lot of time thinking while sitting quietly.  

	One giant paragraph – story shows no formatting choices.
