ESL: Workshop in Composition


Goals

	Demonstrate an understanding of effective strategies for integrating and citing sources.

	Write a problem-solving paper that presents a well-defined thesis, an organized structure, substantial content, and a keen audience awareness.

	Offer critical and constructive feedback to peers and understand the value of receiving feedback from outside respondents.

	Reflect on your writing process and rhetorical effectiveness.



Project Description

1) Problem-solving paper: 
	Your goal is to write a researched paper that demonstrates an interesting and meaningful issue of local or global significance, and propose your solution to the problem. 
	You will integrate secondary research (academic sources) into your report writing. 
	Individually, you will include at least 5 academic sources to inform and persuade your audience. 
	The purposes of the assignment are to build your curiosity as a student researcher, and to connect your research with social issues in local and/or global communities. 
	After completing the project, you will have a group presentation of your researched argument during the last weeks of class.

Problem-solving paper


2) Defining the Problem: 
	Here are some example problems/challenges you could explore. 
	Feel free to come up with your own topic (e.g., using ChatGPT to help you generate a topic).

	• Food desert / Local foods / Access to farmer’s markets / food sustainability / Fast foods vs. nutritious foods available locally  
	• Poverty (Its relationship to education, especially as it relates to area schools)  
	• Revitalization of depressed areas  
	• Increased cost of tuition and textbooks / education spending  
	• Environmental issues: Oil spill and aftermath / DNR new laws on protecting forests / local waste and recycling / air quality / local mining’s effect on ecosystems / urban farming  
	• Oil pipelines and fracking / Energy sustainability  
	• Public Transportation (and the lack thereof)  
	• Issues of illiteracy and access to education  
	• Police militarization, police force racial profiling  
	• Accessibility of campus space to individuals with disabilities and other minority groups  
	• Issues of racism and sexism on campus  
	• Sexual violence / Rape among college students (could address that on multiple scales)  
	• Animal protection / Euthanasia of animals  
	• Issues of immigration  
	• Homelessness  
	• OR a topic of your choice (such as a cross-cultural topic related to your home culture)


Length and Format

	Your final paper should be 1200-1500 words (APA format, doc or docx).  
	Your proposal should be 600-900 words (single spaced; doc or docx).  
	Your reflection essay should be 600-900 words (single spaced; doc or docx).


	The group presentation will mimic the style of conference panel presentations, and your presentation file will be due before the start of your presentation.


Tips and Resources

	You will provide an overview of your problem, offer your solution to the problem (thesis), draft topic sentences relevant to the thesis, and address potential counterarguments.  
	Your problem-solving paper should be based on solid research and carefully curated sources.  
	Use headings/subheadings and simple but memorable introductions and conclusions.  

	Your audience for this researched argument includes your professor and other research writers. 
	Be sure to make your report easily understandable and accessible to the audience. 
	Your audience will assume the authority and trustworthiness of your research. 
	You should consider using styles and voice that convey your authority as a writer, such as using academic language and a first and/or third person, strong tone.

	Keep a record of your report project ChatGPT chat logs. 
	You will submit a brief AI statement that contains links to the chat logs by the end of the semester.


Grading Criteria

	Your project will be graded on the following categories: (Total number of points: 30 pts.)

	• Proposal: Demonstrating your plans for the researched argument; answering all the questions in the prompt; meeting the minimum length and format requirement. (5 pts.)  
	• Rough Draft & Peer Review: Providing constructive feedback on your peer's interview podcasts and sharing how they can improve their work. (2 pts.)  
	• Final Paper: Presenting a well-defined thesis, an organized structure, substantial content, and a keen audience awareness. (15 pts.)  
	• Group Presentation: Presenting your recommendation report in a logical, easy-to-follow manner; incorporating design elements effectively; maintaining eye contact with the audience. (5 pts.)  
	• Reflection Essay: Sharing your writing process and ideas in a clear and thought-provoking manner; meeting the minimum length and format requirement. (3 pts.)
